# Top Tech Jobs in Bulgaria 2026: Salaries, Employers & Market Trends

Bulgaria has quietly become one of Eastern Europe's most attractive tech hubs. With Sofia at its center, a growing pool of talented engineers, competitive salaries relative to cost of living, and access to the EU market, the country is drawing both international companies and ambitious startups.

If you're considering a tech career in Bulgaria—or already working there and curious about market rates—here's what the landscape looks like in 2026.

## Why Bulgaria?

**Sofia as the Tech Hub**  
Sofia accounts for roughly 70% of Bulgaria's tech jobs. The city combines affordable living costs with a vibrant startup scene and established tech offices. Companies benefit from EU membership, a flat 10% income tax rate, and a time zone that overlaps with both Western Europe and the Middle East.

**EU Market Access**  
Bulgaria's EU membership means you can work for clients across the continent without visa friction. Many companies use Bulgaria as a nearshore development center for projects serving Germany, the UK, France, and beyond.

**English Proficiency**  
Most tech roles operate in English. While Bulgarian is the local language, international teams are common, and many engineers are fluent in English from university onward.

## Most In-Demand Roles & Salary Ranges

Here's what employers are hiring for in 2026, with salary ranges in both BGN (Bulgarian Lev) and EUR.

**Software Developer (Full Stack)**  
The bread and butter of Bulgaria's tech economy. Companies want engineers who can handle both frontend (React, Vue) and backend (Node.js, Python, Java).  
- Junior: 3,000–4,500 BGN/month (~1,500–2,300 EUR)  
- Mid-level: 5,000–7,500 BGN (~2,550–3,850 EUR)  
- Senior: 8,000–12,000 BGN (~4,100–6,150 EUR)

**DevOps Engineer**  
Infrastructure automation and cloud deployments (AWS, Azure, GCP) are in high demand. Companies scaling up need engineers who can manage CI/CD pipelines, Kubernetes clusters, and monitoring stacks.  
- Mid-level: 6,000–9,000 BGN (~3,050–4,600 EUR)  
- Senior: 10,000–15,000 BGN (~5,100–7,700 EUR)

**Data Scientist / Machine Learning Engineer**  
Still a hot market. Companies want professionals who can build predictive models, work with large datasets, and deploy ML systems in production.  
- Mid-level: 6,500–9,500 BGN (~3,300–4,850 EUR)  
- Senior: 10,000–16,000 BGN (~5,100–8,200 EUR)

**Product Manager (Technical)**  
As startups mature, they need PMs who understand both user needs and technical constraints. Experience with Agile, data-driven decisions, and technical fluency are key.  
- Mid-level: 5,500–8,000 BGN (~2,800–4,100 EUR)  
- Senior: 9,000–14,000 BGN (~4,600–7,200 EUR)

**UI/UX Designer**  
Design roles are growing. Companies building consumer products need designers who can prototype, run user research, and collaborate with engineers.  
- Mid-level: 4,000–6,500 BGN (~2,050–3,300 EUR)  
- Senior: 7,000–10,000 BGN (~3,600–5,100 EUR)

**Note on Salaries**  
These are base salaries and don't include bonuses, equity, or benefits. Larger companies often add performance bonuses (10–20% of base), while startups may offer equity packages.

## Top Employers Hiring in Bulgaria

**International Tech Companies**  
SAP, VMware, Cisco, HP, and Progress (formerly Telerik) have established offices in Sofia. These companies offer stability, structured career paths, and exposure to enterprise-scale projects.

**Fast-Growing Startups**  
Payhawk (fintech), Gtmhub (OKR software), and Chaos Group (3D rendering) are Bulgarian-born companies that have raised significant funding and are scaling teams.

**Outsourcing & Consultancies**  
MentorMate, Scalefocus, and Questers operate as software houses for international clients. These roles offer variety (different projects every 6–12 months) but less ownership than product companies.

**Remote-First Companies**  
GitLab, Automattic, and Toptal hire Bulgarian engineers for remote roles. You get international salaries (often higher than local rates) while living in Sofia.

## Remote vs. Office: What's the Split?

**Hybrid is the Default**  
Most Bulgarian tech companies adopted hybrid models post-pandemic. Expect 2–3 days in the office and 2–3 days remote. Fully remote roles exist but are less common than in Western Europe.

**Remote Work for International Roles**  
If you're working for a US or Western European company, remote work is more standard. Many engineers in Sofia work fully remote for companies based in London, Berlin, or San Francisco.

**Office Culture Still Matters**  
Bulgarian startups often emphasize in-person collaboration for early-stage teams. If you're joining a small company (<50 people), expect more office time than a 500-person enterprise.
